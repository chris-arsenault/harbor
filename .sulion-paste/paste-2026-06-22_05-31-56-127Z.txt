**Additional OANDA data beyond candles.** OANDA has two genuinely distinctive feeds that are directly relevant to a liquidity-sweep thesis:

- **Order Book** (`/v3/instruments/{instrument}/orderBook`) and **Position Book** (`/v3/instruments/{instrument}/positionBook`) — OANDA's own clients' resting orders and open positions, bucketed by price. This is *exactly* a map of where liquidity and stops are clustered, which is the entire premise of your strategy. Use it to (a) prefer sweeps of levels that sit on a visible order/position cluster, and (b) target the next cluster as the draw. Big caveat: these are ~20-minute snapshots and OANDA doesn't serve deep history, so for backtesting you'd have to start recording them now and build your own history. Use them live/forward, study them going forward.
- **Bid/ask candles + tick volume** — already in the candles endpoint. Tick volume (OANDA's "volume" is tick count, not real volume) is a usable displacement/participation filter: a sweep on a volume spike behaves differently than a quiet one.
- **Pricing stream** for true spread over time (filter out wide-spread regimes) and finer granularity (S5) for more precise sweep/FVG detection and fill modeling.
- OANDA's **Forex Labs** sentiment/order-book data (long/short positioning ratios) — softer, but a contrarian-positioning filter is in the same liquidity family.

**Similar strategies (same liquidity family) worth testing as variants.** Your tideline is essentially an intraday **"turtle soup"** (Larry Williams' false-breakout reversal) — read that ruleset, it's the canonical version of what you're doing and may sharpen your entry. Others in the same space: **PDH/PDL sweep** (previous day high/low are stronger pools than session extremes), **Asian-range breakout/fade** (London sweeps the Asian range — a classic), and the highest-value swap: **require a market-structure shift (MSS/BOS) after the sweep before entering**, instead of a bare FVG. Sweep → break of the most recent swing → *then* enter on the FVG/order block. The structure-shift confirmation typically filters out the sweeps that just keep going. Also consider **order-block entries** as an alternative to FVG, and targeting **VWAP/session-VWAP** reversion rather than a fixed RR.

**Search-space changes — but add priors, not dimensions.** The instinct is to widen the space; that worsens overfit. Better moves:

- *Decouple exit from entry and test it hard* — this is usually where the money is. Fixed 1:2 is arbitrary. Test partial-at-1R + runner, ATR trailing, time stop (exit after N minutes regardless), and "exit at the next liquidity pool."
- Add *filters as categorical params with strong priors*: HTF-bias gate (only take sweeps aligned with the daily/4H draw), volatility regime (ATR bucket), day-of-week mask (Mon/Fri often differ), and a news filter. Each is one binary dimension but encodes real structure.
- Refine the sweep definition itself: minimum penetration depth, minimum rejection-candle size relative to ATR, and *which* levels qualify (only PDH/PDL + session extremes that coincide with an order-book cluster).
- Tighten, don't loosen: a strategy that needs a sprawling search space to look good is usually overfit. Fewer, better-motivated parameters generalize.
